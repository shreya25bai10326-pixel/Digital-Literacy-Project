# CaseStudy: Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking details. These attacks usually occur through emails, messages, calls or fake websites.

In this case, Ankit, a first-year college student, received an email that appeared to be from his bank. The email warned him that her account would be temporarily blocked due to “suspicious activity” unless he verified his details immediately. It included a link that looked similar to the bank’s official website.

Concerned about losing access to his account, Ankita clicked on the link and was redirected to a fake login page. Without noticing the slight difference in the website URL, he entered his username, password, and OTP. Within minutes, the attackers gained access to her bank account and performed unauthorized transactions.

Phishing attacks commonly target students, working professionals, and elderly users who may not carefully verify the authenticity of emails or links. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and compromised personal data.

This case highlights the importance of checking email authenticity, verifying website URLs, and never sharing confidential information through unknown links. Users should directly visit official websites or contact customer support to confirm any urgent requests.
