What is Digital Literacy?

- Ability to find, understand, create, and share information using digital devices and the internet in a safe and responsible way.
- Includes using tools like email, search engines, cloud storage, and online learning platforms effectively.
- Involves critical thinking: checking if information is trustworthy, identifying fake news, and understanding how algorithms and recommendations may influence what you see online.
- For students, it means using technology not just for entertainment, but for learning, collaboration, and building a professional online identity.
  ​
Useful Digital Tools for Students

- Productivity: Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Slides), Microsoft 365, Notion, to organise notes, assignments, and group work.
- Learning: MOOCs and platforms like Coursera, edX, NPTEL, YouTube EDU channels for extra explanations and skill-building.
- Coding & Projects: GitHub for version control and portfolio, HackerRank / CodeChef for practice, Kaggle for data science projects.
- Communication: College email, Microsoft Teams / Google Meet / Zoom, and discussion forums for interacting with faculty and peers in a professional way.
  ​
Safe Internet Practices

- Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication for important accounts.
- Think before you click: avoid suspicious links in emails, SMS, and social media; do not download unknown attachments.
- Protect your privacy: limit what personal data you share publicly, review app permissions, and check social media privacy settings regularly.
- Be respectful and responsible online: avoid cyberbullying, verify information before sharing, and follow college policies and laws related to digital behaviour.
